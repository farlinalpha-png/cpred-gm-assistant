What is This?

Hello, choombas! If you’re reading this DLC in the 
present (January 2025) your eyeballs (or ears) are 
latched onto a preview of The Edgerunner’s Guide 
to Night City, our upcoming sourcebook all about 
our favorite Dark Future city for Cyberpunk RED. 
If you’re reading this from the future (after the 
book’s release) consider this DLC to be a taste test of 
The Edgerunner’s Guide to Night City with a 
gorgeous map and a list of locations to use in your 
Cyberpunk RED campaign.
Those locations, by the way, come from everything 
we’ve released thus far for Cyberpunk RED, up to 
Tales of the RED: Hope Reborn. There are over 
one hundred and fifty of them … but that’s just a fraction 
of the total. There are hundreds more coming in The 
Edgerunner’s Guide to Night City.
This DLC comes in two parts. The first is this PDF, 
which contains a map of Night City spread across two 
pages (for printing on 8.5x11 paper) and brief summaries about the districts and (some of) the locations 
in city. The second is a ZIP file containing four versions 
(in PDF and JPG formats) of the map: Night City with 
all locations marked, Night City with no locations and 
no district labels, and Night City with no locations but 
with district labels, and Night City with everything. Use 
the one you prefer! 
By the way, stick around to the end for a bonus 
surprise!
About the Map
about t h e m a p about t h e m a p
When looking at the map, don’t try to match it oneto-one with the Night City of Cyberpunk 2020 or 
Cyberpunk 2077. From a 2020 point of view, Night 
City has expanded, both in the fictional world it is part 
of and in the imaginations of its creators into a larger 
and noisier place. When it comes to 2077, we recognized the limitations of a video game world created a 
representational version of a city big enough to feel 
real but small enough for players to drive across in a 
few minutes without getting bored.
Which is why, for this map, there are more streets. A 
lot more. Some of the names come from Cyberpunk 
2077 and even the real-world Morro Bay, but they 
won’t always be an exact match. Some of the changes 
come from “filling in the map.” Others simply represent 
Night City’s existence as a metropolis in constant flux, 
rebuilding itself on the regular as it recovers from war, 
natural disasters, gang and terrorist attacks, and edgerunners (like your Crew) blowing shit up.
The Night City of 2045
t h e ni g h t c i tyof2045
The residents of Night City mentally divide their home 
into four distinct areas: The Island, the Northside, the 
Mainland, and the Southside. Each area contains 
multiple districts that are part of Night City but also 
locally governed by their City Manager, who usually 
represents the interests of the area’s major powers.
NCPD has jurisdiction over the entire city, but City 
Managers are allowed to hire and deputize a secondary security force. These deputized security forces have 
police powers, including the right to investigate crimes 
and arrest and detain suspects. 
Major roadways (marked in yellow on the map) are 
of special interest to not only the city government but 
the state of NorCal and the Pacific Confederation since 
they represent major routes through Night City. They are 
maintained, even in combat zones, and might be patrolled 
by local security, NCPD, or possibly NorCal Marshalls.
Extra Districts?
e xtradistricts e xtradistricts
Clever readers might notice more districts on this map 
of Night City than were on the map in the Cyberpunk 
RED core rulebook. That’s by design. Over the past 
few years, as we’ve worked on the game’s story, we’ve 
found spaces where the city has expanded naturally. 
Santo Domingo and North Heywood were more or 
less divided from the beginning. Kabuki split off as we 
worked on Danger Gal Dossier. The Reclamation 
Zone was born from the story of Heywood as told in 
the Cyberpunk: Edgerunners Mission Kit.
Other districts came into being as questions arose 
while planning out The Edgerunner’s Guide to 
Night City. Can we gentrify an already somewhat 
gentrified area? Absolutely! Bring on Downtown. 
Where does upper middle management live while 
working their way into a grave or the Exec Zone?

Charter Hill. Would the Thelas nomads run the port as
